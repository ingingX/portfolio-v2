# Analyzing Human vs. Neural Attention in VQA

## Problem

This master’s thesis investigates the similarities and differences between human visual attention and transformer-based attention mechanisms in Visual Question Answering (VQA) systems, aiming to improve the interpretability and trustworthiness of multimodal AI models.

## Methodology

The project combines transformer-based VQA architectures, attention-map analysis, and human attention comparison workflows to analyze how neural networks process visual and linguistic information under different question-answering conditions.

## Contribution

This work strengthened my interests in explainable AI, human-centered AI systems, multimodal reasoning, and trustworthy AI research, and later inspired my ongoing research on gaze-guided multimodal evaluation systems.
